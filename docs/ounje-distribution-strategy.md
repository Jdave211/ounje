# Ounje Distribution Strategy

Research date: 2026-05-13

Goal: shift Ounje distribution away from product-first posting and toward content that starts with meals people already want: TikTok recipes, saved recipe videos, restaurant orders, and weekly meal prep cravings.

## Core Insight

The strongest Ounje content is not "meal planning app" content. It is transformation content:

- restaurant photo -> home recipe
- saved TikTok -> clean recipe
- recipe screenshot -> grocery list
- fridge photo -> dinner idea
- craving -> weekly meal plan
- meal photo -> shopping path

Sovian Discover is strongest for hook language. The highest-signal posts do not begin with a clean product value prop. They start with delayed discovery, disbelief, or regret:

- "am I late or is this actually new?"
- "why did nobody tell me about this?"
- "no way I have been saving all these recipes this whole time"
- "I can actually use all the recipes I have been saving?"
- "save it, share it, make it tonight"

The useful pattern is: open with a social-discovery feeling, show the food or saved video first, then reveal Ounje as the bridge from craving to recipe, grocery list, and checkout.

## ReelFarm Takeaway

ReelFarm confirms that mobile-app distribution should be slideshow/listicle native, not polished product-demo native. Strong examples skew toward:

- confession-style hooks
- listicles: "5 ways", "3 foods", "signs you..."
- relatable food/meal-prep visuals
- soft-sell CTAs where the app feels discovered, not advertised
- repeatable formats that can be automated across many hooks and meal anchors

The existing Ounje-adjacent ReelFarm drafts should not be used as-is. They are too broad. They talk about takeout, meal planning, restaurant dependency, and recipe apps, but they do not make the actual Ounje magic obvious enough: photo or saved food video in, usable recipe or meal plan out.

Use them only as negative reference.

## Creative Bar

Every Ounje distribution piece needs to pass this test:

- Can the viewer understand the input in the first two seconds?
- Can the viewer see what Ounje does with that input?
- Is there a specific CTA tied to their own behavior?

If a post only says "5 ways I stopped meal planning burnout" or "5 ways I stopped being takeout dependent," it is too abstract. The hook can be emotional, but the body has to show the pipeline.

## CTA Rules

Weak CTAs:

- "try Ounje"
- "download Ounje"
- "meal plan smarter"
- "stop ordering takeout"

Better CTAs:

- "Take a photo of the meal and put it in Ounje."
- "Send your next saved recipe video to Ounje."
- "Drop the restaurant order into Ounje before you forget it."
- "Use Ounje on the food video you keep meaning to cook."
- "Before you reorder it, turn it into a recipe in Ounje."
- "Screenshot the meal, import it, and let Ounje build the grocery list."

## Hook Rules

Use hooks that feel like a friend realizing something late:

- Am I late or did everyone know this?
- Why did nobody tell me about this?
- No way this has been possible the whole time.
- I have been saving food videos for years and just found this.
- This changes how I plan dinner.
- Wait, so this can become groceries?
- I thought I just had to remember this meal.
- I am not gatekeeping this.

Avoid product-first hooks:

- "Ounje turns restaurant meals into recipes."
- "Use this app to generate grocery lists."
- "AI meal planning app for restaurant meals."
- "Save money by recreating restaurant meals."

Those are fine as the payoff, not the first line.

## Operating Plan

For Sovian:

- Save examples that demonstrate hook language, not just exact product fit.
- Prioritize creators comfortable filming selfie-style food discovery, share sheet flows, and actual meals.
- Use creator application fields for niche, base rate, best video, partner/friend availability, and selfie/photo quality.

For ReelFarm:

- Do not use generic lifestyle slideshows unless the pipeline appears by slide 2.
- Build slideshow automations around visible transformations:
  - "3 restaurant meals I would put into Ounje"
  - "4 food videos I finally turned into real dinners"
  - "5 meals I photographed so Ounje could build the recipe"
  - "3 things in my saved folder that became groceries"
  - "5 takeout orders I turned into home versions"
- Keep slides visually food-first, then show the Ounje output.
- Use CTA endings that instruct behavior:
  - "Take a photo of the meal and put it in Ounje."
  - "Send your next saved recipe video to Ounje."
  - "Before you reorder it, let Ounje make the home version."
