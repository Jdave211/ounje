# Ounje Creator Briefs

Research date: 2026-05-13

Use these as the production briefs. Each one must show the pipeline clearly:

1. Food input: saved TikTok, meal photo, restaurant order, caption, fridge, or craving.
2. Ounje receiving that input.
3. Ounje output: recipe, grocery list, cart path, or meal plan.
4. Clear CTA tied to the viewer's own behavior.

## Brief 1: Saved TikTok Recipe Rescue

Anchor: a creator has hundreds of saved food videos and never actually makes them.

Hook options:

- "am I late or can you actually use all the food videos you saved?"
- "why did nobody tell me my saved recipes could become dinner?"
- "no way I have been screenshotting recipes this whole time"
- "I found the app for people with a graveyard of saved recipes"

Story:

1. Open on the creator scrolling saved TikTok recipes, with one craveable recipe visible.
2. Say the hook while lingering on the food video, not the app.
3. Share/import the recipe video into Ounje.
4. Show Ounje extracting or organizing the recipe.
5. Show the grocery list or cart path.
6. End with the actual meal or ingredients on the counter.

Creator direction:

- Keep the first two seconds about the saved video, not the app.
- Make the app reveal feel like "I just found this."
- Show one specific recipe, not a generic app tour.

CTA:

- "Send the recipe to Ounje before you forget it again."
- "Use Ounje on the food video you keep meaning to cook."

## Brief 2: Restaurant Meal Memory

Anchor: a restaurant meal the creator cannot stop thinking about becomes a make-at-home plan.

Do not lead with "turn restaurant meals into recipes." Lead with the same discovery language as the recipe-app posts.

Hook options:

- "am I late or can dinner out become next week's meal prep?"
- "why did nobody tell me I could save a restaurant meal like this?"
- "no way I was just supposed to remember this order forever"
- "I thought this was just a restaurant thing"
- "been ordering this for years and now I make a version at home?"
- "wait, so the meal I keep craving can become groceries?"
- "I am not gatekeeping how I copied my favorite order"
- "this is what I do now when a restaurant meal is too good"

Default hook:

"am I late or can dinner out become next week's meal prep?"

Alternate hook:

"why did nobody tell me I could save a restaurant meal like this?"

Story:

1. Open with a clear photo/video of the restaurant meal: table shot, takeout box, menu name, or "still thinking about this."
2. Say the hook as an overheard thought, not a product pitch.
3. Show the actual input: the creator uploads a meal photo, screenshot, or typed restaurant order into Ounje.
4. Show Ounje turning it into a home recipe direction: protein, sauce, carb, toppings, spice level, sides.
5. Show the grocery list or cart path.
6. End with the home version plated or the groceries on the counter.

Creator direction:

- Film this like a food memory, not a software demo.
- The meal should be specific: "spicy salmon bowl", "shawarma plate", "birria tacos", "miso mushroom pasta", "jerk chicken rice bowl".
- The pipeline must be visible: food photo/order -> Ounje -> recipe/groceries -> cooked meal.
- Avoid claims that it perfectly duplicates a restaurant's proprietary recipe.

CTA:

- "Take a photo of the meal and put it in Ounje."
- "Before you reorder it, turn it into a recipe in Ounje."
- "Drop the restaurant order into Ounje before you forget it."

Required visual beats:

- Beat 1: the restaurant meal, full-screen.
- Beat 2: the phone photo or screenshot.
- Beat 3: Ounje receiving the photo/order.
- Beat 4: generated recipe or grocery list.
- Beat 5: home version, grocery haul, or ingredients.

## Brief 3: Recipe Caption Chaos

Anchor: TikTok captions are messy, long, incomplete, or spread across comments.

Hook options:

- "why are recipe captions always like a scavenger hunt?"
- "am I late or is there a cleaner way to save these?"
- "no way I used to pause the video 12 times for ingredients"

Story:

1. Show a viral recipe caption packed with ingredients and steps.
2. Show the creator trying to screenshot/pause.
3. Import the video, screenshot, or caption to Ounje.
4. Show clean ingredients, steps, and grocery list.
5. Show the creator cooking from the cleaned-up recipe.

CTA:

- "Screenshot the recipe mess and let Ounje clean it up."

## Brief 4: Meal Prep From Cravings

Anchor: meal prep usually feels boring, but Ounje starts with food people actually crave.

Hook options:

- "I do not want meal prep, I want the food I keep saving."
- "why did nobody tell me meal prep could start from cravings?"
- "am I late or can my saved food videos become the plan?"

Story:

1. Show three saved meals or recent restaurant orders.
2. Put the photos/videos into Ounje as the week's anchors.
3. Show Ounje organizing them into a realistic plan and grocery list.
4. End with prep containers that still look craveable.

CTA:

- "Plan from the food you actually want to eat. Put the photos in Ounje."

## Brief 5: Fridge-To-Dinner

Anchor: leftovers and random groceries become a real meal instead of waste.

Hook options:

- "am I late or can my fridge tell me dinner?"
- "why did nobody tell me these random groceries were a meal?"
- "no way I almost ordered takeout with this much food at home"

Story:

1. Open the fridge or pantry.
2. List 4-6 ingredients already on hand.
3. Ask Ounje for a recipe that uses them.
4. Show the meal and the smaller grocery add-on list.

CTA:

- "Check Ounje before you order takeout."

## Brief 6: Grocery Cart Proof

Anchor: make the payoff tangible by showing Ounje moves from recipe to grocery action.

Hook options:

- "wait, so the recipe can become a cart?"
- "why did nobody tell me dinner planning could end here?"
- "I thought saving recipes was the hard part. It was the groceries."

Story:

1. Start with a meal anchor from TikTok or a restaurant.
2. Show Ounje organizing ingredients.
3. Show the grocery list/cart handoff.
4. End with the ingredients or meal, not a checkout-heavy screen.

CTA:

- "Go from craving to cart."
