# Ounje Distribution Briefs

Research date: 2026-05-13

Goal: shift Ounje distribution away from product-first posting and toward content that starts with meals people already want: TikTok recipes, saved recipe videos, restaurant orders, and weekly meal prep cravings.

## What The Research Shows

Sovian Discover is strongest for recipe-app hook language. The highest-signal posts do not begin with a clean product value prop. They start with delayed discovery, disbelief, or regret:

- "am I late or is this actually new?"
- "why did nobody tell me about this?"
- "how did I last years screenshotting recipes?"
- "been cooking for years and now I find this?"
- "no way I have been saving all these recipes this whole time"
- "I can actually use all the recipes I have been saving?"
- "save it, share it, make it tonight"

The useful pattern is: open with a social-discovery feeling, show the food or saved video first, then reveal Ounje as the bridge from craving to recipe, grocery list, and checkout.

ReelFarm confirms that mobile-app distribution should be slideshow/listicle native, not polished product-demo native. Strong examples in the mobile-app and healthy-eating database skew toward:

- confession-style hooks
- listicles: "5 ways", "3 foods", "signs you..."
- relatable food/meal-prep visuals
- soft-sell CTAs where the app feels discovered, not advertised
- repeatable formats that can be automated across many hooks and meal anchors

ReelFarm Library has several Ounje-adjacent slideshow drafts, but they should not be used as-is:

- "5 ways i stopped being a takeout dependent"
- "5 ways i finally stopped doom-scrolling recipe apps."
- "5 ways i finally stopped doom-scrolling recipe apps?"
- "5 boundaries that started my batch cooking era:"
- "5 ways i stopped feeling guilty about simple dinners"
- "5 shifts i made to stop dreading kitchen cleanup"
- "5 ways i stayed consistent with my meal rotation"
- "5 micro-habits that helped me ditch restaurant dependency"
- "5 ways i stopped spiraling over dinner planning"
- "5 ways i stopped being intimidated by authentic recipes"
- "5 shifts that made me stop meal planning burnout (fast)"
- "5 ways i stopped abandoning half-finished cookbooks"

These are too broad. They talk about takeout, meal planning, restaurant dependency, and recipe apps, but they do not make the actual Ounje magic obvious enough: photo or saved food video in, usable recipe or meal plan out. They also lack a hard CTA.

Use them only as negative reference. The next generation of Ounje content needs a clearer transformation:

1. Start with a specific food photo, TikTok recipe, restaurant order, or craving.
2. Show Ounje receiving that input.
3. Show the output: recipe, grocery list, cart path, or meal plan.
4. End with a direct CTA telling the viewer what to import into Ounje.

## Creative Bar

Every Ounje distribution piece needs to pass this test:

- Can the viewer understand the input in the first two seconds?
- Can the viewer see what Ounje does with that input?
- Is there a specific CTA tied to their own behavior?

If a post only says "5 ways I stopped meal planning burnout" or "5 ways I stopped being takeout dependent," it is too abstract. The hook can be emotional, but the body has to show the pipeline.

The strongest Ounje content is not "meal planning app" content. It is transformation content:

- restaurant photo -> home recipe
- saved TikTok -> clean recipe
- recipe screenshot -> grocery list
- fridge photo -> dinner idea
- craving -> weekly meal plan
- meal photo -> shopping path

## CTA Rules

Weak CTAs:

- "try Ounje"
- "download Ounje"
- "meal plan smarter"
- "stop ordering takeout"

Better CTAs:

- "Take a photo of the meal and put it in Ounje."
- "Send your next saved recipe video to Ounje."
- "Drop the restaurant order into Ounje before you forget it."
- "Use Ounje on the food video you keep meaning to cook."
- "Before you reorder it, turn it into a recipe in Ounje."
- "Screenshot the meal, import it, and let Ounje build the grocery list."

## Hook Rules

Use hooks that feel like a friend realizing something late:

- Am I late or did everyone know this?
- Why did nobody tell me about this?
- No way this has been possible the whole time.
- I have been saving food videos for years and just found this.
- This changes how I plan dinner.
- Wait, so this can become groceries?
- I thought I just had to remember this meal.
- I am not gatekeeping this.

Avoid product-first hooks:

- "Ounje turns restaurant meals into recipes."
- "Use this app to generate grocery lists."
- "AI meal planning app for restaurant meals."
- "Save money by recreating restaurant meals."

Those are fine as the payoff, not the first line.

## Brief 1: Saved TikTok Recipe Rescue

Anchor: a creator has hundreds of saved food videos and never actually makes them.

Hook options:

- "am I late or can you actually use all the food videos you saved?"
- "why did nobody tell me my saved recipes could become dinner?"
- "no way I have been screenshotting recipes this whole time"
- "I found the app for people with a graveyard of saved recipes"

Story:

1. Open on the creator scrolling saved TikTok recipes, with one craveable recipe visible.
2. Say the hook while lingering on the food video, not the app.
3. Share/import the recipe video into Ounje.
4. Show Ounje extracting or organizing the recipe.
5. Show the grocery list or cart path.
6. End with the actual meal or ingredients on the counter.

Creator direction:

- Keep the first two seconds about the saved video, not the app.
- Make the app reveal feel like "I just found this."
- Show one specific recipe, not a generic app tour.

CTA:

- "Send the recipe to Ounje before you forget it again."
- "Use Ounje on the food video you keep meaning to cook."

## Brief 2: Restaurant Meal Memory

Anchor: a restaurant meal the creator cannot stop thinking about becomes a make-at-home plan.

The old hook should not be too literal. Do not lead with "turn restaurant meals into recipes." Lead with the same discovery language as the recipe-app posts.

Hook options:

- "am I late or can dinner out become next week's meal prep?"
- "why did nobody tell me I could save a restaurant meal like this?"
- "no way I was just supposed to remember this order forever"
- "I thought this was just a restaurant thing"
- "been ordering this for years and now I make a version at home?"
- "wait, so the meal I keep craving can become groceries?"
- "I am not gatekeeping how I copied my favorite order"
- "this is what I do now when a restaurant meal is too good"

Story:

1. Open with a clear photo/video of the restaurant meal: table shot, takeout box, menu name, or "still thinking about this."
2. Say the hook as an overheard thought, not a product pitch.
3. Show the actual input: the creator uploads a meal photo, screenshot, or typed restaurant order into Ounje.
4. Show Ounje turning it into a home recipe direction: protein, sauce, carb, toppings, spice level, sides.
5. Show the grocery list or cart path.
6. End with the home version plated or the groceries on the counter.

Creator direction:

- Film this like a food memory, not a software demo.
- The meal should be specific: "spicy salmon bowl", "shawarma plate", "birria tacos", "miso mushroom pasta", "jerk chicken rice bowl".
- The pipeline must be visible: food photo/order -> Ounje -> recipe/groceries -> cooked meal.
- Avoid claims that it perfectly duplicates a restaurant's proprietary recipe.

CTA:

- "Take a photo of the meal and put it in Ounje."
- "Before you reorder it, turn it into a recipe in Ounje."
- "Drop the restaurant order into Ounje before you forget it."

Required visual beats:

- Beat 1: the restaurant meal, full-screen.
- Beat 2: the phone photo or screenshot.
- Beat 3: Ounje receiving the photo/order.
- Beat 4: generated recipe or grocery list.
- Beat 5: home version, grocery haul, or ingredients.

## Brief 3: Recipe Caption Chaos

Anchor: TikTok captions are messy, long, incomplete, or spread across comments.

Hook options:

- "why are recipe captions always like a scavenger hunt?"
- "am I late or is there a cleaner way to save these?"
- "no way I used to pause the video 12 times for ingredients"

Story:

1. Show a viral recipe caption packed with ingredients and steps.
2. Show the creator trying to screenshot/pause.
3. Import the video, screenshot, or caption to Ounje.
4. Show clean ingredients, steps, and grocery list.
5. Show the creator cooking from the cleaned-up recipe.

CTA:

- "Screenshot the recipe mess and let Ounje clean it up."

## Brief 4: Meal Prep From Cravings

Anchor: meal prep usually feels boring, but Ounje starts with food people actually crave.

Hook options:

- "I do not want meal prep, I want the food I keep saving."
- "why did nobody tell me meal prep could start from cravings?"
- "am I late or can my saved food videos become the plan?"

Story:

1. Show three saved meals or recent restaurant orders.
2. Put the photos/videos into Ounje as the week's anchors.
3. Show Ounje organizing them into a realistic plan and grocery list.
4. End with prep containers that still look craveable.

CTA:

- "Plan from the food you actually want to eat. Put the photos in Ounje."

## Brief 5: Fridge-To-Dinner

Anchor: leftovers and random groceries become a real meal instead of waste.

Hook options:

- "am I late or can my fridge tell me dinner?"
- "why did nobody tell me these random groceries were a meal?"
- "no way I almost ordered takeout with this much food at home"

Story:

1. Open the fridge or pantry.
2. List 4-6 ingredients already on hand.
3. Ask Ounje for a recipe that uses them.
4. Show the meal and the smaller grocery add-on list.

CTA:

- "Check Ounje before you order takeout."

## Brief 6: Grocery Cart Proof

Anchor: make the payoff tangible by showing Ounje moves from recipe to grocery action.

Hook options:

- "wait, so the recipe can become a cart?"
- "why did nobody tell me dinner planning could end here?"
- "I thought saving recipes was the hard part. It was the groceries."

Story:

1. Start with a meal anchor from TikTok or a restaurant.
2. Show Ounje organizing ingredients.
3. Show the grocery list/cart handoff.
4. End with the ingredients or meal, not a checkout-heavy screen.

CTA:

- "Go from craving to cart."

## Distribution Operating Plan

For Sovian:

- Save examples that demonstrate hook language, not just exact product fit.
- Prioritize creators comfortable filming selfie-style food discovery, share sheet flows, and actual meals.
- Use creator application fields for niche, base rate, best video, partner/friend availability, and selfie/photo quality.

For ReelFarm:

- Do not use generic lifestyle slideshows unless the pipeline appears by slide 2.
- Build slideshow automations around visible transformations:
  - "3 restaurant meals I would put into Ounje"
  - "4 food videos I finally turned into real dinners"
  - "5 meals I photographed so Ounje could build the recipe"
  - "3 things in my saved folder that became groceries"
  - "5 takeout orders I turned into home versions"
- Keep slides visually food-first, then show the Ounje output.
- Use CTA endings that instruct behavior:
  - "Take a photo of the meal and put it in Ounje."
  - "Send your next saved recipe video to Ounje."
  - "Before you reorder it, let Ounje make the home version."

## Brief 2 Final Hook Recommendation

Use this as the default Brief 2 hook:

"am I late or can dinner out become next week's meal prep?"

Use this as the alternate:

"why did nobody tell me I could save a restaurant meal like this?"

These keep the restaurant brief aligned with the rest of the hook set while still making the restaurant-to-recipe angle clear by the third beat.
